How to Update Your Email Signature and Default Font

Watch the 3-minute how-to video →

Quick steps:

  1. Settings > Accounts > Signatures > "+ Add signature".
  2. Copy your block from the Word doc and paste it in.
  3. Fix spacing so it matches, then check "Set as Default".
  4. Set default font to Verdana under File > Settings > Mail > Compose and Reply.
Need more detail? Full written steps

Signature setup:

  1. Go to Settings (gear icon top right of screen) > Accounts > Signatures
  2. Click the blue button "+ Add signature".
  3. Enter a name such as "Official Signature".
  4. Navigate to the Word document.
  5. Scroll through the signatures and find yours.
  6. Hover over it until you see a small square above and to the left.
  7. Click on the square, then move the mouse just below it.
  8. Right-click and select copy.
  9. Navigate back to Outlook and paste into the signature field.
  10. Hover below the Y's web address.
  11. When you get the double arrows, drag down so it matches the spacing.
  12. At the bottom, select the checkbox for "Set as Default…".

Default font setup:

  1. Go to File > Settings > Mail > Compose and Reply.
  2. Under "Message Format," click the font drop down and select Verdana.
  3. Click the blue "Save" button.
